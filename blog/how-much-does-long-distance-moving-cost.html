How Much Does Long Distance Moving Cost in 2025?

December 8, 2025 8 min read

Planning a long distance move? One of the first questions you'll ask is "how much will this cost?" The answer varies widely - from $2,000 to $15,000 or more - depending on several factors. This comprehensive guide breaks down everything you need to know about long distance moving costs in 2025.

Average Long Distance Moving Costs

According to industry data, the average cost for a long distance move in 2025 ranges from:

  • Studio/1-bedroom apartment: $1,500 - $3,500
  • 2-bedroom home: $3,000 - $6,000
  • 3-bedroom home: $4,500 - $9,000
  • 4+ bedroom home: $6,000 - $15,000+

These ranges are broad because traditional movers charge based on weight, distance, and services - creating unpredictable final bills.

Factors That Affect Moving Costs

1. Distance

The farther you move, the more you'll typically pay. Traditional movers charge per mile, which can add up quickly on cross-country moves.

2. Weight/Volume of Items

Most interstate movers charge by weight. A typical 3-bedroom home weighs 7,500-10,000 pounds. At $0.50-$0.70 per pound, that's $3,750-$7,000 just for the weight component.

3. Time of Year

Peak moving season (May-September) costs 20-30% more than off-season moves. Weekends and end-of-month dates are also premium times.

4. Additional Services

Extra services that increase costs include:

  • Full-service packing: $500-$2,500+
  • Specialty item handling (pianos, antiques): $150-$500 per item
  • Storage: $100-$300 per month
  • Extra stops: $50-$200 each

5. Access Challenges

Stairs, long carries from the truck, and elevator-only buildings can add $75-$200+ to your bill.

Hidden Costs to Watch For

Traditional moving quotes often exclude costs that appear later:

  • Shuttle service: If a big truck can't access your street, a smaller truck adds $300-$500
  • Long carry fees: Distance from truck to door over 75 feet costs extra
  • Stair fees: $50-$100 per flight
  • Bulky item fees: Extra charges for large furniture
  • Waiting time: Delays at pickup or delivery can be billed hourly

Flat Rate vs. Traditional Moving Pricing

There are two main pricing models for long distance moves:

Traditional (Weight + Distance) Pricing

Pros: May be cheaper for very small moves

Cons: Unpredictable final cost, potential for add-on charges, often includes warehouse stops

Flat Rate Pricing

Pros: Know your exact cost upfront, no surprise charges, often includes direct delivery

Cons: May cost more for very small moves

How to Save Money on Long Distance Moving

1. Move During Off-Peak Times

Moving in winter months (November-February) or mid-week can save 20-30%.

2. Declutter Before Moving

Every pound costs money with traditional movers. Sell, donate, or discard items you don't need.

3. Get Multiple Quotes

Always get at least 3 quotes. Compare apples-to-apples - make sure quotes include the same services.

4. Consider Flat Rate Moving

For larger homes, flat rate moving often saves thousands compared to weight-based pricing.

5. Pack Yourself (If Able)

Professional packing adds $500-$2,500. If you have time, pack non-fragile items yourself.
